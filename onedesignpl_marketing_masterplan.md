# PART 2: THE CONTENT MACHINE
## What to Post, How to Post It, and Why It Works

---

## 2.1 THE ALGORITHM — HOW IT ACTUALLY WORKS

Instagram's algorithm is not mysterious. It is a machine that answers one question: **"Will this content keep people on Instagram longer?"**

If your content keeps people watching, saving, sharing, and commenting — the algorithm rewards you by showing it to more people. If people scroll past it in 0.3 seconds, the algorithm buries it.

There are five signals the algorithm uses to decide your fate, ranked by importance:

**1. DM Shares (most powerful)**
When someone sends your Reel or post to a friend via DM, Instagram interprets this as: "This content is so good someone wanted to share it privately." This is the strongest signal for reaching people who don't follow you yet. Design content specifically to make people think "I need to send this to someone."

**2. Saves**
When someone saves your post, Instagram interprets this as: "This content is valuable enough to keep." Saves drive reach more than likes. Carousels with useful information ("5 mistakes", "how to choose X") consistently get saved. Every educational post you make should have save-worthy information.

**3. Watch Time / Retention**
For Reels, the percentage of the video people watch matters more than the number of views. A Reel where 80% of viewers watch to the end will beat a Reel with 10× more views but 20% retention. This is why your first 1.5 seconds are the most important 1.5 seconds of your business.

**4. Comments**
Comments signal engagement and discussion. Posts that ask specific, easy-to-answer questions get more comments. "Który wariant wolicie — A czy B?" is better than "Co myślicie?"

**5. Likes (least important)**
Likes still matter but they are the weakest signal. Don't optimize for likes.

**The implication:** Make content designed to be shared, saved, and watched completely. Everything else follows.

---

## 2.2 THE CONTENT PILLARS

You will post content across three pillars, rotating throughout the week. Each pillar serves a different purpose in your funnel.

---

### PILLAR 1: DISCOVERY CONTENT
**Goal:** Reach people who don't know you exist yet.
**Formats:** Reels (primary), carousels with bold hooks.
**How it works:** This content is designed to be shared and to have high watch time. It reaches new audiences through the algorithm and through shares.

**Best performing types for interior design:**
- Before and after transformations
- "Unbelievable" reveals — rooms that look impossible
- Time-lapses of a room going from empty to finished
- "Rate my space" reactions — you react to spaces sent by followers
- Renovation mistake reveals — "This is what happens when you choose the wrong contractor"
- Trend reactions — "Here's why I would never put this in a client's home"

**The key insight about discovery content:** It must create an emotional reaction in under 2 seconds. Curiosity, surprise, or desire. If it doesn't do that, it won't get shared and it won't reach new people.

---

### PILLAR 2: TRUST CONTENT
**Goal:** Convert new followers into warm leads who trust you.
**Formats:** Carousels (primary), long-form Reels (30–60s), Stories.
**How it works:** This content demonstrates expertise, saves well, and builds the perception that you are the smartest people in the room.

**Best performing types for interior design:**
- "X mistakes people make when renovating their [room]"
- "How to choose the right [material/color/contractor]"
- "What to know before you start a renovation"
- "Why your apartment feels small and how to fix it"
- "The questions you should ask every interior designer before hiring them"
- Behind-the-scenes process content — showing the thinking, not just the result
- Material mood boards with explanations of why you chose each element
- "Project breakdown" carousels — one project, explained decision by decision

**The key insight about trust content:** People don't hire you because you showed them a pretty room. They hire you because they believe you will solve their problems and not waste their money. This content creates that belief.

---

### PILLAR 3: CONVERSION CONTENT
**Goal:** Turn warm followers into paying clients and customers.
**Formats:** Stories (primary), carousels, Reels with soft CTAs.
**How it works:** This content is directed at people who already follow you and already trust you. It reminds them that you are available and removes the friction from reaching out.

**Best performing types:**
- Client testimonials — video preferred, text with a project photo works too
- "Here's what working with us looks like" — the full process from first call to finished space
- "We have 2 spots open for Q3 renovations" — scarcity, real or planned
- Q&A Stories — you answer real questions from followers about their renovation challenges
- DM invitation Stories — "Send me a photo of your problem space and I'll tell you what I would do"
- Result posts with a direct CTA: "If you want this for your apartment, link in bio"

**The key insight about conversion content:** People need an invitation. Most potential clients are sitting there thinking "I'd like to work with them but I don't want to bother them." Your job is to make reaching out feel easy, obvious, and normal.

## 2.3 THE WEEKLY SCHEDULE

Six posts per week. This is the minimum for meaningful growth on Instagram in 2026. Less than this and the algorithm deprioritizes your account. More than this and you sacrifice quality.

Here is your permanent weekly schedule:

---

**MONDAY — Discovery Reel**
The hardest hitting content of the week. Your best before/after, your most dramatic transformation, your most surprising result. This is your reach content. Spend the most time on Monday's content. The hook must be written and rewritten until it's perfect.

**TUESDAY — Trust Carousel**
An educational carousel. 7–10 slides. Slide 1 is the hook — a bold claim or a question that creates curiosity or concern. Slides 2–9 deliver on the promise with specific, useful information. Slide 10 is a soft CTA. This is your save-driving content.

**WEDNESDAY — Discovery Reel**
Your second Reel of the week. Can be slightly softer than Monday — a process video, a material walkthrough, a "day in the life of a project" format. Still needs a strong hook. Still needs to be shareable.

**THURSDAY — Stories Only**
No feed post. Only Stories. Use this day to be human and interactive. Behind the scenes. A poll. A question box. Show yourself. This builds the parasocial relationship that makes people loyal. Three to five Stories minimum.

**FRIDAY — Trust Carousel or Conversion Post**
Alternate week by week. One week, a trust carousel. The next week, a conversion post (client testimonial, "our process," or availability announcement).

**SATURDAY — Short Discovery Reel**
7 to 15 seconds. A quick tip. A design hack. Something people will share with a friend. Low production value is fine on Saturdays — authenticity and speed matter more than polish here.

**SUNDAY — Rest**
Never post on Sunday. The algorithm doesn't reward it and you need one day to plan the next week.

## 2.4 THE REEL FORMULA

Every single Reel you make must follow this structure. Memorize it.

---

### SECOND 0–1.5: THE HOOK
This is everything. Fifty percent of viewers decide whether to keep watching within 1.5 seconds. If you lose them here, the rest of your production quality means nothing.

**The hook has two components:**
1. **Visual hook** — movement, a reveal, something visually unexpected. Never start a Reel with a static shot. The video must be moving from frame one.
2. **Text hook** — three to eight words on screen that create an irresistible need to know more.

**Hook categories that work:**

*Curiosity hooks:*
- "Ten błąd kosztuje ludzi tysiące złotych"
- "Dlaczego twoje mieszkanie wygląda tanio"
- "Czego żaden architekt ci nie powie"

*Transformation hooks:*
- "48m² → dom marzeń (zanim/po)"
- "To samo mieszkanie. 3 miesiące różnicy."
- "Wyglądało tragicznie. Teraz nie do poznania."

*Controversy/opinion hooks:*
- "Szary jest przereklamowanym kolorem i mam dowody"
- "Przestań kupować te rzeczy do swojego mieszkania"
- "Dlaczego nie ufam większości architektów wnętrz"

*Pain point hooks:*
- "Planujesz remont? Najpierw obejrzyj to."
- "Zanim podpiszesz umowę z wykonawcą..."
- "Twoje mieszkanie jest małe? Nie, po prostu źle urządzone."

---

### SECOND 1.5–25: THE CONTENT
Show, don't tell. Let the visuals do the work. If you're talking, make sure your words are adding something the visuals don't communicate on their own.

Rules for the content section:
- **Subtitles on everything.** Eighty-five percent of Instagram is watched without sound. If your content only works with sound, you're losing most of your audience.
- **Cut aggressively.** Every second that doesn't add value removes viewers. When in doubt, cut it.
- **Movement every 2–3 seconds.** Change the shot, add text, use a transition. Keep the eye moving.
- **Face on camera when possible.** Video with a face in it retains 35% more viewers. You don't have to talk to camera. Even a brief shot of you pointing at something, holding a material sample, or walking through a space dramatically increases retention.

---

### LAST 3 SECONDS: THE CTA
Never end a Reel without telling people what to do next. One clear action. Not two. One.

Options depending on the goal:
- "Planujesz remont? Napisz do nas — link w bio"
- "Zapisz to na później" (for educational content — drives saves)
- "Wyślij to komuś, kto planuje mieszkanie"
- "Masz pytania? Pisz w komentarzach"

---

## 2.5 THE CAROUSEL FORMULA

Carousels are your trust-building engine. They get saved more than any other format. A saved carousel works for you for months — people come back to it, reshare it, show it to friends.

---

**SLIDE 1: THE HOOK SLIDE**
Bold text. Large font. A claim or question that makes someone stop scrolling. The image matters less than the text here. Examples:
- "5 błędów, które sprawią że twój remont będzie koszmarem"
- "Dlaczego twoje mieszkanie wygląda jak hotel a nie jak dom"
- "Zanim kupisz kanapę, przeczytaj to"

**SLIDES 2–8: THE VALUE SLIDES**
Each slide delivers one piece of the promised information. One point per slide. Short text. Supporting image. The information must be genuinely useful — not vague, not general. Specific enough that someone reads it and thinks "I didn't know that."

**SLIDE 9 (SECOND TO LAST): THE TENSION SLIDE**
Before you end, restate the stakes. Remind them why this matters. "Jeśli zignorujesz te zasady, remont będzie kosztował dwa razy tyle." This keeps people engaged through to the last slide.

**SLIDE 10: THE CTA SLIDE**
One clear action. Usually: "Zapisz, żebyś miał to pod ręką gdy zaczniesz remont" or "Napisz do nas jeśli chcesz żebyśmy to zrobili za ciebie."

---

## 2.6 CAPTION STRATEGY

Most people underwrite captions. They write one sentence and an emoji. This is a mistake because the caption is where you build the relationship and where the algorithm reads your content for categorization.

**Caption structure:**

**Line 1:** Your hook — the same energy as your Reel or carousel hook. This is what shows before "more" and determines whether anyone reads further.

**Lines 2–5:** The story or the context. Why does this project matter? What problem did the client have? What was the challenge? What decision did you make and why?

**Lines 6–8:** The teaching moment. What can someone reading this take away even if they never hire you?

**Last line:** Engagement question or CTA. Make it specific and easy to answer. "Który kolor byś wybrał — ciepły szary czy chłodny biały?" is better than "Co myślicie?"

**Hashtag approach:**
Place hashtags in the first comment, not the caption. This keeps captions clean. Use 10–15 hashtags. Split them three ways:
- 4–5 niche Polish hashtags with under 100,000 posts: #projektowaniewnetrzwarszawa #wnetrzaminimalistyczne #mieszkaniedosnu
- 4–5 mid-size hashtags with 100,000 to 500,000 posts: #projektwnetrz #dekoracjewnetrz #architekturawnetrz
- 2–3 broader hashtags with under 2,000,000 posts: #wnetrza #interiordesignpoland

Never use #interiordesign (200+ million posts). Your content will drown in noise.

# PART 3: GROWTH TACTICS
## How to Build an Audience Without Spending a Fortune

## 3.1 THE DAILY 30-MINUTE GROWTH RITUAL

Every day, before you post anything, you spend 30 minutes doing this. Skipping this is the single biggest growth mistake small accounts make.

**10 minutes — Comment on competitor accounts:**
Go to the five largest Polish interior design accounts. Find their three most recent posts. Leave a genuine, specific, insightful comment. Not "Piękne ✨". Something like: "Wybór tkanin do tej sofy to strzał w dziesiątkę — ta faktura idealnie rozbija zimne tony ściany." This comment gets seen by everyone who visits that post. Many of those people will click your profile. Some will follow you.

**10 minutes — Engage with your followers:**
Reply to every comment on your posts. Reply to every DM within the day it arrives. Like and comment on stories from followers who engage with yours. The algorithm rewards accounts with high response rates. More importantly, followers who feel seen become loyal followers who tell others.

**10 minutes — Tag and engage with brands/suppliers:**
Every material, every piece of furniture, every paint color you use in a project — tag the brand. Leave a comment on their latest post mentioning you used their product in a recent project. Brands repost this content constantly. A single repost from a brand with 50,000 followers can bring you 200 new followers in a day.

## 3.2 THE COLLABORATION STRATEGY

Collaboration is the fastest free growth strategy available to you. Here's exactly how to execute it.

**Who to collaborate with:**

*Real estate agents* — They show apartments to people who will soon renovate. Their audience is perfectly aligned with yours. Propose a collab post: "We designed this apartment while it was listed for sale — here's the before and after." Both accounts post it as a joint post. Both audiences see it.

*Mortgage brokers* — People getting a mortgage are buying a home they will furnish and potentially renovate. Same logic as real estate agents.

*Furniture stores and interior suppliers* — Tag them in every post that features their products. Email them directly: "Hi, we used your [product] in a recent project. Here are the photos — would you like to reshare?" Most will say yes. Many will propose a paid collaboration later.

*Photographers* — Offer to credit the photographer in every project photo in exchange for them promoting the collaboration to their audience. Professional photographers who shoot interiors have audiences of design-conscious people.

*General contractors and builders* — They need a designer they trust to recommend to their clients. You need contractors you trust. Build a referral relationship and cross-promote on Instagram.

**How to propose a collaboration:**

Send a DM. Keep it short. Here is a template:

"Cześć [Name], jestem [Name] z OneDesign — projektujemy wnętrza w Warszawie. Zauważyłem/am że pracujemy dla podobnych klientów. Czy byłbyś/byłabyś zainteresowany/a wspólnym postem lub Stories, które pokazałoby nasze projekty Twojej publiczności i odwrotnie? Mam konkretny pomysł jeśli chcesz pogadać."

Follow up once after 5 days if no response. Never follow up twice.

## 3.3 HOW TO USE PAID PROMOTION INTELLIGENTLY

With 500–1,500 PLN per month, you cannot run a brute-force ad campaign. You have to be surgical. Here is exactly what to do.

**Rule 1: Never boost a post that isn't already performing.**
Boosting a weak post just wastes money. Wait 48 hours after every Reel posts. If it's getting organic reach and engagement, that is the one you boost. If it's flat, don't touch it.

**Rule 2: Boost for reach, not conversions.**
At your current stage, your goal is follower growth and brand awareness, not direct sales. Set your boost objective to "More profile visits" or "More reach" — not "Messages" or "Website visits." You want people to discover your profile and follow you.

**Rule 3: Targeting.**
Your boost targeting should be:
- Location: Warsaw + 30km radius (for potential clients) + all of Poland (for general audience growth)
- Age: 26–45
- Interests: Home renovation, interior design, architecture, real estate

**Rule 4: Budget split.**
- 500 PLN: Boost one Reel per month. Run it for 10 days at 50 PLN/day.
- Remaining 500–1,000 PLN: Invest in one professional content shoot per month. This is more valuable than more ad spend at this stage.

**Rule 5: The professional shoot priority.**
Right now your biggest constraint is video content. One 2-hour shoot at a completed project with a videographer produces:
- 4–6 short Reels (raw footage + B-roll)
- 1 longer walkthrough video
- 15–20 still photos with professional quality
This is 3–4 weeks of content from one shoot. This investment compounds. Prioritize it above ad spending.

## 3.4 THE STORIES STRATEGY

Stories are chronically underused by design studios. Here is why they matter and how to use them.

Stories are viewed primarily by your existing followers. They do not directly drive follower growth. But they do three critical things:

**1. They build the parasocial relationship.**
When people watch your Stories regularly, they feel like they know you. They feel invested in your work. This is what converts a passive follower into someone who recommends you to their friend who is renovating.

**2. They feed the algorithm.**
Instagram measures how many of your followers interact with your Stories. High Story interaction tells the algorithm your account is engaging, which improves your Reel reach.

**3. They are your most direct sales channel.**
People who watch Stories are warm leads. A simple Story saying "We have one slot open for a project starting in September — DM us if interested" will convert better than any feed post CTA.

**Your Stories schedule:**

*Monday:* Share Monday's Reel to Stories with a poll ("Który styl wolisz — A czy B?" — two images from the project). This drives both Story interaction and Reel views.

*Wednesday:* Behind the scenes. A photo or video from your current project. No editing needed. Raw and real. "Jesteśmy dziś na realizacji — wybieramy ostateczny odcień ściany."

*Thursday:* Full interactive sequence. Three to five Stories:
- Question box: "Jakie masz pytanie o remoncie/urządzaniu mieszkania?"
- Poll: Something fun or relevant to your current project
- Personal moment: Coffee, the office, your day — something human

*Friday:* Answer questions from Thursday's question box in Stories. This creates a loop — people ask questions Thursday, watch for answers Friday. High retention.

*Saturday:* Reshare your Saturday Reel to Stories.

*Sunday:* Nothing, or a single "week recap" — a quick collage of the week's content.

# PART 4: THE MONETIZATION BLUEPRINT
## Four Revenue Streams, Built in Order

---

## 4.1 STREAM 1: CLIENT ACQUISITION (START IMMEDIATELY)

This is your primary revenue source and the reason you built the business. Instagram should be generating 3–6 qualified consultation inquiries per month by month 3.

**How to make this happen:**

Every single piece of content you post should have either a soft or direct CTA pointing toward a consultation. Here is the language to use:

*Soft CTA (for discovery and trust content):*
"Planujesz remont? Napisz do nas — link w bio."
"Bezpłatna konsultacja — szczegóły w bio."

*Direct CTA (for conversion content):*
"Mamy otwarte miejsca na projekty od [month]. Napisz do nas zanim zajmiemy harmonogram."
"Wyślij nam zdjęcia swojego mieszkania przez DM — powiem ci co bym tam zmienił/a."

**The "DM your space" tactic:**
Once a week, post a Story saying: "Wyślij mi zdjęcie swojego salonu przez DM — dam ci 3 konkretne sugestie co zmienić." Respond to every DM genuinely. Do not try to sell in the first message. Just help them. After you've given advice, say: "Jeśli chcesz żebyśmy zajęli się tym kompleksowo, mogę się z tobą umówić na bezpłatną konsultację." 

Conversion rate on this tactic is typically 15–25%. If 20 people DM you, 3–5 will book a consultation.

**Your bio must have a booking link.**
Use Calendly (free). Create a "15-minute free consultation" appointment type. Put the link in your bio. Remove every other link. The simpler the path, the more bookings you get.

---

## 4.2 STREAM 2: AFFILIATE MARKETING (START AT 500 FOLLOWERS)

Every project you complete is a shopping opportunity for your audience. They see your completed interior and they want to recreate it. Affiliate marketing lets you earn a commission every time they buy something you recommended.

**How it works:**
You create special links for every product in your projects. When someone clicks your link and buys the product, you earn 3–12% commission. The buyer pays the same price they would pay directly. You earn money for a recommendation you would have made anyway.

**Where to set this up:**

*LTK (formerly LikeToKnow.it):*
This is the primary platform interior designers use. You create a profile, link products from any online store, and share those links in your posts. Your followers can shop exactly what they see in your photos. Application is free. LTK takes a percentage but the commission rates are typically 8–15%.

*Amazon.pl Storefront:*
Create an Amazon influencer storefront and organize products into collections: "My favorite lamps," "Best budget sofa options," "Materials I always recommend." Link this in your bio alongside your consultation booking.

*Direct affiliate programs:*
Many Polish furniture and home stores have their own affiliate programs. Contact your most-used suppliers directly and ask if they have one. Often they don't advertise it but they will offer commission deals to accounts that consistently promote their products.

**How to integrate this into your content:**

Every project showcase post should include a comment or caption with: "Wszystkie produkty z tego projektu znajdziecie w linku w bio." Make the shopping easy and immediate. People have a short window of desire — if they can't find the product in 10 seconds, they move on.

**Realistic income at different follower levels:**
- 1,000 followers, engaged audience: 200–500 PLN/month
- 5,000 followers: 800–2,000 PLN/month
- 20,000 followers: 3,000–8,000 PLN/month

These numbers assume consistent product placement in content. If you only link products occasionally, income will be lower.

## 4.3 STREAM 3: DIGITAL PRODUCTS (START AT 1,000 FOLLOWERS)

You have knowledge that people will pay for. Not just about design — about the renovation process, contractor management, material selection, avoiding costly mistakes. Package this knowledge once, sell it forever.

---

**PRODUCT 1: "Przewodnik Remontowy" PDF (Price: 39–59 PLN)**
A comprehensive guide to planning a renovation in Poland. What to do before you start, how to choose and manage contractors, what to budget for, what mistakes to avoid. 20–30 pages. Created once in Canva. Sold on Gumroad.

Why this works: People planning a renovation are anxious and looking for certainty. A 49 PLN guide that saves them 5,000 PLN in mistakes is an obvious purchase. Your Instagram content demonstrates that you know what you're talking about — when you offer the guide, trust is already established.

**PRODUCT 2: Moodboard Templates for Canva (Price: 49–79 PLN)**
A pack of 10 professional moodboard templates that homeowners or designers can use to plan their spaces. Created once in Canva. Sold on Etsy Poland or Gumroad. This is genuinely passive income — once made, it sells indefinitely.

Why this works: People who follow interior design accounts on Instagram often love design but can't afford a designer. They want tools to do it themselves. You provide the tools without competing with your own services (the people who buy templates are not your clients — your clients hire you to do the thinking).

**PRODUCT 3: Mini-Course "Jak urządzić mieszkanie bez architekta" (Price: 199–349 PLN)**
A 4–6 module video course covering: how to choose a style, how to select a color palette, how to plan furniture layout, how to choose materials, how to work with light. Hosted on Podia or Teachable. Recorded on a phone — the quality of information matters more than production quality.

Why this works: This is your highest revenue product per sale. At 299 PLN, selling 20 units per month generates 5,980 PLN. Your Instagram content builds the authority that makes people willing to pay for your knowledge.

**How to sell digital products through Instagram:**
- Mention the product in the caption of relevant posts: "Mam cały przewodnik po tym procesie — link w bio"

# PART 6: MINDSET
## What Separates Accounts That Explode From Accounts That Plateau

---

## 6.1 THE COMPOUNDING REALITY

Your first month will feel like shouting into a void. Your first Reel might get 200 views. Your first carousel might get 50 likes. This is normal and it is not a signal to stop or change strategy. It is a signal to stay consistent.

Instagram growth is not linear. It is exponential — but only after a critical mass of consistent content. The accounts at 100K followers today mostly spent 6–12 months posting consistently to a very small audience before anything broke through.

Every post you make is an asset. An educational carousel you posted in month 1 will continue to get saves and traffic for the next 18 months. A Reel that seemed to underperform can get picked up by the algorithm weeks after posting if something changes in the virality signals. Your content library is a business asset that compounds over time.

Do not stop because it feels slow.

---

## 6.2 THE ONE THING THAT WILL ACTUALLY MAKE YOU EXPLODE

All the tactics above are important. But there is one thing that, when it happens, will grow your account faster than all of them combined: **a viral Reel.**

A viral Reel for an account your size means 50,000–500,000+ views. This can happen to any Reel on any day. It is not fully in your control. But you can dramatically increase the probability by doing the following:

**Create content specifically designed to be shared between friends.**
The DM share is the viral mechanism. Not going on the Explore page. Not trending audio. Someone watching your Reel and immediately thinking "I need to send this to [specific person]."

The content most likely to be DM shared is:
1. Content that solves a specific, relatable problem ("Twoje mieszkanie wygląda małe bo robisz ten błąd")
2. Content that reveals a surprising truth ("Dlaczego meble z IKEA wyglądają drożej niż są")
3. Content that validates something people secretly believe but don't say out loud ("Remont pod klucz jest wart każdej złotówki i tu jest dlaczego")
4. Content that is so useful someone wants to save it AND send it: checklists, decision frameworks, "before you buy" guides

When you make each piece of content, ask yourself: "Is there a specific person someone would send this to?" If yes, you're on the right track. If no, think about how to make the angle more specific.

---

## 6.3 WHAT TO DO WHEN A REEL GOES VIRAL

If a Reel suddenly gets 100,000+ views, you have a narrow window to convert that attention into followers and clients. Do this immediately:

1. **Post a follow-up Reel within 48 hours** — same topic, different angle. People who liked your viral Reel will watch the follow-up. "You asked about X — here's what I didn't have time to say"

2. **Reply to every comment within 24 hours** — the comment section of a viral post is full of potential clients and collaborators. Treat every comment like a personal introduction.

3. **Pin the viral Reel to your profile** — it's the first thing new profile visitors will see.

4. **Update your bio** — make sure your consultation booking link is visible and the CTA in your bio is as clear and direct as possible. Viral traffic converts better when the path is obvious.

5. **Don't change your strategy** — the instinct after a viral post is to chase the same exact format obsessively. Resist this. The viral Reel worked because you were being authentic and strategic. Go back to your schedule and keep building.

---

## 6.4 THE METRICS THAT MATTER AND THE ONES THAT DON'T

**Metrics that matter:**
- Reel reach (people who don't follow you seeing your content)
- Saves per post (quality of educational content)
- Profile visits per week (interest generated by discovery content)
- Consultation requests per month (business conversion)
- Follower growth rate week over week

**Metrics that don't matter:**
- Total follower count (at small scale)
- Likes (weakest algorithmic signal)
- Impressions (this includes your existing followers seeing your posts — not growth)
- Comments that are just emojis

Check your analytics every Sunday. Two numbers only: which posts drove the most new profile visits this week, and which posts got the most saves. Make more of those two things.
